Program Overview

This five-course graduate certificate provides a comprehensive foundation in generative AI theory, methods, and applications. Students engage with foundational concepts in machine learning, develop competency in system design and deployment, and complete an applied capstone integrating coursework into a substantive project.

The program is designed to accommodate working professionals, with courses offered in evening and online formats across two to three semesters.

Coursework reflects current advances in generative AI research, with readings drawn from peer-reviewed publications and active areas of inquiry.

Curriculum

Five graduate courses spanning foundational theory, applied methods, and integrative design.

GENAI 501 · Course 1

Foundations of Generative AI

Core concepts underlying large language models, diffusion models, and transformer architectures. Tokenization, embedding representations, attention mechanisms, and mathematical foundations.

3 Credits · Fall Semester
GENAI 502 · Course 2

AI-Augmented Workflows & Automation

Design of intelligent automation pipelines using APIs, prompt engineering, and orchestration frameworks for document processing, information retrieval, and decision support.

3 Credits · Fall Semester
GENAI 601 · Course 3

Retrieval-Augmented Generation & Agent Frameworks

In-depth study of chains, agents, memory architectures, and RAG. Students build research assistants and domain-specific question-answering systems.

3 Credits · Spring Semester
GENAI 602 · Course 4

Advanced AI Systems & Architecture

Production-grade AI systems: scalability, evaluation methodology, safety, fine-tuning, multi-agent orchestration, MLOps, and responsible AI frameworks.

3 Credits · Spring Semester
GENAI 690 · Course 5

Capstone: Applied AI System Design

Substantial applied project in collaboration with an industry or research partner. Design, implementation, evaluation, and formal presentation of an end-to-end AI system.

3 Credits · Spring Semester

Research & Scholarship

Sample student research topics and scholarly contributions from the certificate program.

Clinical Decision Support via RAG

Retrieval-augmented generation framework for clinical decision support, presented at the AAAI 2026 Workshop on AI in Healthcare. Ongoing collaboration with Michigan Medicine.

Multi-Agent Code Review Systems

Multi-agent system for automated code review at scale. Research presented at ICSE 2026; system integrated into production engineering workflows.

Bias Propagation in Generative Text

Investigation of bias propagation in generative text models, contributing to ongoing work on AI fairness. Findings published in FAccT 2026 proceedings.

RAG Evaluation Benchmarks

Open-source evaluation benchmark for retrieval-augmented generation systems, adopted by several academic research groups. Presented at NeurIPS 2025 Datasets & Benchmarks track.

Academic & Professional Outcomes

Graduates of this certificate are prepared to:

AI Research & Development

Design, train, and evaluate generative AI models in academic or applied research settings. Contribute to peer-reviewed research in NLP, ML systems, and AI safety.

ML Systems Engineering

Architect and maintain scalable AI infrastructure. Apply MLOps practices for production deployment, monitoring, and cost optimization.

Intelligent Automation Design

Design and implement AI-augmented workflows and organizational processes using APIs, orchestration frameworks, and agent systems.

AI Governance & Policy

Advise organizations on responsible AI practices, governance frameworks, and regulatory compliance. Conduct ethical assessments of AI systems.

Applied NLP Engineering

Develop and deploy language model applications for domain-specific use cases including RAG systems, conversational agents, and document processing.

Further Graduate Study

Pursue doctoral research or advanced master's work in computer science, information science, or related fields with a strong AI foundation.

Admissions Requirements

Prerequisites

  • Bachelor's degree from an accredited institution
  • Minimum cumulative GPA of 3.0 on a 4.0 scale
  • Familiarity with Python programming (or completion of the department's preparatory module)
  • Basic knowledge of statistics and linear algebra is recommended

Application Materials

  • Completed online application via the Rackham Graduate School
  • Official transcripts from all post-secondary institutions
  • Statement of purpose (500–750 words)
  • Current résumé or CV
  • One letter of recommendation (academic or professional)

Deadlines

  • Fall admission: June 1 (priority); July 15 (final)
  • Spring admission: November 1 (priority); December 1 (final)

Tuition & Financial Support

  • Tuition for the 15-credit certificate: $12,500
  • Graduate financial aid, departmental assistantships, and employer tuition support may be available
  • Contact the Office of Financial Aid for details

Frequently Asked Questions

Can certificate credits be applied toward a graduate degree?▾
Credits earned in the certificate program may be applied toward select master's degree programs at the University of Michigan, subject to review and approval by the receiving department. Students considering this pathway are encouraged to consult with their academic advisor.
Are there opportunities for research involvement?▾
Students may pursue independent study or research assistantship opportunities with program faculty, subject to availability. The capstone course (GENAI 690) also provides a structured framework for applied research projects.
What is the expected time commitment?▾
Full-time students typically complete the certificate in two semesters. Part-time students may extend enrollment to three semesters. Each 3-credit course requires approximately 8–10 hours per week, including lectures, readings, and project work.
Is the program available fully online?▾
All courses are offered in both in-person (evening) and synchronous online formats. Online students participate in the same lectures, discussions, and project experiences as on-campus students.
