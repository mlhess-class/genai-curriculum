Program Overview

This five-course graduate certificate provides a comprehensive foundation in generative AI theory, methods, and applications. Students engage with foundational concepts in machine learning, develop competency in system design and deployment, and complete an applied capstone integrating coursework into a substantive project.

The program is designed to accommodate working professionals, with courses offered in evening and online formats across two to three semesters.

Curriculum

Five graduate courses spanning foundational theory, applied methods, and integrative design.

GENAI 501

Foundations of Generative AI

Introduces core concepts underlying large language models, diffusion models, and transformer architectures. Topics include tokenization, embedding representations, attention mechanisms, and the mathematical foundations of modern generative systems.

3 CreditsFall Semester
GENAI 502

AI-Augmented Workflows & Automation

Examines the design of intelligent automation pipelines using APIs, prompt engineering, and orchestration frameworks. Students develop and evaluate AI-integrated workflows for domains including document processing, information retrieval, and decision support.

3 CreditsFall Semester
GENAI 601

Retrieval-Augmented Generation & Agent Frameworks

Provides an in-depth study of the LangChain ecosystem and related frameworks — including chains, agents, memory architectures, and retrieval-augmented generation. Students build and evaluate applications such as research assistants and domain-specific question-answering systems.

3 CreditsSpring Semester
GENAI 602

Advanced AI Systems & Architecture

Addresses the design of production-grade AI systems, covering scalability, evaluation methodology, safety considerations, fine-tuning, and multi-agent orchestration. Additional topics include MLOps practices, deployment strategies, and responsible AI frameworks.

3 CreditsSpring Semester
GENAI 690

Capstone: Applied AI System Design

Students undertake a substantial applied project in collaboration with an industry or research partner. The capstone requires the design, implementation, evaluation, and formal presentation of an end-to-end AI system addressing a defined problem.

3 CreditsSpring Semester

Frequently Asked Questions

Academic policies, prerequisites, and program details.

What are the prerequisite requirements?▾
Applicants should hold a bachelor's degree from an accredited institution. Familiarity with Python programming is recommended. Students without prior programming experience may fulfill the prerequisite through the department's preparatory module offered before the fall semester.
Can certificate credits be applied toward a graduate degree?▾
Credits earned in the certificate program may be applied toward select master's degree programs at the University of Michigan, subject to review and approval by the receiving department. Students considering this pathway are encouraged to consult with their academic advisor.
Are there opportunities for research involvement?▾
Students in the certificate program may pursue independent study or research assistantship opportunities with program faculty, subject to availability. The capstone course (GENAI 690) also provides a structured framework for applied research projects.
What is the expected time commitment?▾
Full-time students typically complete the certificate in two semesters. Part-time students may extend enrollment to three semesters. Each 3-credit course requires approximately 8–10 hours per week, including lectures, readings, and project work.
Is the program available fully online?▾
All courses are offered in both in-person (evening) and synchronous online formats. Online students participate in the same lectures, discussions, and project experiences as on-campus students.
What are the tuition and financial aid options?▾
Tuition for the 15-credit certificate is $12,500. Students may be eligible for graduate financial aid, departmental assistantships, or employer tuition support. Contact the Office of Financial Aid for details on available funding.
