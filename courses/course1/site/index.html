Course Description

This course provides a comprehensive, hands-on introduction to generative AI for students with no prior technical background. You will learn how large language models work, develop practical prompt engineering skills, explore real-world applications across industries, and critically examine the ethical implications of AI-generated content. By the end of this course you will be a confident, thoughtful, and effective user of generative AI tools.

Learning Objectives

  • Explain the core concepts behind large language models, including transformers, tokens, and attention
  • Use prompt engineering techniques to get high-quality outputs from AI systems
  • Critically evaluate AI outputs for accuracy, bias, and appropriateness
  • Apply generative AI to writing, analysis, coding, and professional workflows
  • Articulate the ethical, legal, and societal implications of generative AI
  • Design a personal AI toolkit tailored to your academic and professional goals

Weekly Schedule

1

What is AI? History & Context

From Turing to transformers — the 80-year journey to modern AI. Symbolic AI, machine learning, deep learning, and the generative revolution.

2

How LLMs Work

Transformers, tokens, attention mechanisms, and training at scale — explained without the math. How prediction becomes conversation.

3

Hands-On: ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini

Comparing leading models side-by-side. Strengths, weaknesses, pricing, and when to use each. HW 1 Due

4

Prompt Engineering Fundamentals

Clarity, specificity, role-setting, and output formatting. The anatomy of an effective prompt.

5

Advanced Prompting Techniques

Chain-of-thought, few-shot learning, system prompts, and iterative refinement. HW 2 Due

6

AI for Writing, Summarization & Analysis

Drafting, editing, summarizing long documents, sentiment analysis, and structured extraction.

7

AI for Code Generation & Debugging

Using AI as a coding partner — generating, explaining, and debugging code even if you're not a programmer. HW 3 Due

8

Midterm Exam & Review

In-class midterm covering Weeks 1–7. Review session on Tuesday, exam on Thursday. MIDTERM

9

Ethics of AI: Bias, Fairness & Transparency

Algorithmic bias, training data problems, fairness frameworks, and the black-box problem.

10

Academic Integrity & AI Policies

Where the line is: acceptable use, citation, disclosure, and institutional policies. HW 4 Due

11

AI Across Industries

Healthcare, law, finance, education, creative arts — how generative AI is transforming every sector.

12

Limitations: Hallucinations & Reasoning Failures

What AI gets wrong, why, and how to detect and mitigate errors. HW 5 Due

13

The AI Landscape: Open vs Closed, Local vs Cloud

Open-source models (Llama, Mistral), API economics, privacy trade-offs, and running models locally.

14

Building Your AI Toolkit

Designing a personal workflow — choosing tools, creating templates, and integrating AI into your daily work. HW 6 Due

15

Final Presentations & Wrap-Up

Student presentations of AI toolkit projects. Course reflection and looking ahead. FINAL

Homework Assignments

HW 1 — AI First Impressions

Due: Week 3. Compare outputs from three AI models on identical prompts. Reflect on differences.

HW 2 — Prompt Engineering Lab

Due: Week 5. Design and test prompts using basic and advanced techniques. Document your iterative process.

HW 3 — AI-Assisted Writing & Code

Due: Week 7. Use AI to draft a document and generate code. Critically evaluate the outputs.

HW 4 — Ethics Case Study

Due: Week 10. Analyze a real-world AI ethics case. Propose a framework for responsible use.

HW 5 — Hallucination Audit

Due: Week 12. Systematically test AI models for factual errors. Document failure patterns.

HW 6 — Personal AI Toolkit Design

Due: Week 14. Design and document your ideal AI-enhanced workflow for your field.

Course Policies

AI Usage Policy

You are encouraged to use AI tools in this course — it's what we're studying. However, you must always disclose when and how AI was used in any submission. Undisclosed AI use is an academic integrity violation. The goal is to learn to use AI well, not to have AI do your learning for you.

Late Work

Assignments lose 10% per day late, up to 3 days. After 3 days, no credit without an approved extension. Extensions must be requested at least 24 hours before the deadline.

Attendance

Attendance is expected. More than 3 unexcused absences will result in a participation grade penalty. If you must miss class, let me know in advance.

Accessibility

If you need accommodations, please contact the Office of Disability Services and share your letter with me in the first two weeks. I'm committed to making this course accessible to everyone.
