# HW4 Answer Key: Ethics Case Study

**For TA Use Only**

---

## Part 1: Case Selection & Research (10 points)

**Minimum requirements:**
- 3 credible sources (academic, major news, official reports)
- 400-500 word summary
- Must include: what happened, who was affected, current status/outcome

**Source quality check:**
- Blog posts and opinion pieces alone are insufficient
- Wikipedia is okay as starting context but shouldn't count as one of the 3 sources
- AI-generated "research" without real URLs is an automatic 0 on this section — verify at least one source

**Grading by case:**

### CNET AI Articles
- Key facts: 77 articles published Jan 2023, errors found by Futurism, no byline disclosure initially
- Affected: readers, CNET journalists, public trust in media
- Outcome: CNET paused AI articles, added disclosure, several corrections issued

### Deepfake Political Ads
- Key facts: AI-generated robocall mimicking Biden (Jan 2024 NH primary), various campaign ads globally
- Affected: voters, democratic process, public trust
- Outcome: FCC ruled AI-generated voice calls illegal, state-level legislation emerging

### AI Art & Copyright
- Key facts: Class action suits by artists (Andersen v. Stability AI, 2023), Getty v. Stability AI
- Affected: visual artists, AI companies, creative industry
- Outcome: Cases still progressing; US Copyright Office ruled purely AI-generated art not copyrightable

### Amazon Hiring Tool
- Key facts: Developed 2014, found to penalize resumes mentioning "women's" (e.g., "women's chess club"), scrapped 2018
- Affected: female applicants, HR processes
- Outcome: Tool abandoned; became landmark case study in algorithmic bias

### AI Student Submissions
- Key facts: Turnitin launched AI detection 2023, false positives reported (esp. ESL students), professor wrongly accused students
- Affected: students (especially non-native English speakers), academic integrity processes
- Outcome: Many universities revising policies; some dropped AI detectors due to unreliability

### GitHub Copilot
- Key facts: Trained on public GitHub repos, lawsuit filed Nov 2022, questions about GPL/license compliance
- Affected: open-source developers, software industry, licensing frameworks
- Outcome: Ongoing litigation; Copilot added license filters; broader debate about training data rights

## Part 2: Ethical Analysis (20 points, 10 per framework)

**For each framework, look for:**

1. **Correct application** of the framework (not just using the name but actually reasoning through it)
2. **Specific application** to their case (not generic ethics discussion)
3. **Student's own assessment** of whether they agree with the framework's conclusion
4. **Nuance** — acknowledging that ethical frameworks can reach different conclusions

**Common mistakes:**
- Confusing utilitarianism with "whatever benefits the most people" without considering harm magnitude
- Applying deontological ethics as just "is it legal?" (legality ≠ morality)
- Virtue ethics that's just character attacks rather than structural analysis
- Justice framework that doesn't identify specific populations disproportionately affected

**Red flag:** Ethical analysis that reads like an AI-generated essay. These tend to be balanced to the point of saying nothing. Push for the student's actual position.

## Part 3: Policy Proposal (15 points)

**Strong proposals:**
- Target a specific entity (a university, a platform, a government agency)
- Include concrete mechanisms (not just "companies should be more responsible")
- Address enforcement (who checks compliance? what are consequences?)
- Consider unintended consequences (e.g., over-regulation stifling innovation, disclosure requirements burdening small creators)

**Grading:**
- 13-15: Specific, actionable, addresses objections, realistic
- 10-12: Good idea but vague on enforcement or ignores objections
- 7-9: Very general or impractical
- <7: Incomplete or naive

## Part 4: Reflection (5 points)

- Must show genuine engagement, not just "this was an interesting assignment"
- Strong answers identify a specific tension or trade-off they wrestled with
- **Full credit** requires naming the hardest ethical trade-off specifically

## General TA Notes

- This assignment has the highest risk of AI-generated submissions. Read the ethical analysis carefully — AI tends to produce perfectly balanced, committee-approved-sounding analysis. Student work should have a point of view.
- If a student's "3 credible sources" include URLs that don't work, flag for instructor review (possible AI-fabricated citations).
