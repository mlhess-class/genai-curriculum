## Learning Objectives

- Use AI as a collaborative writing tool while maintaining authorial voice
- Explore AI-assisted code generation even without programming experience
- Critically evaluate AI-generated content for quality, accuracy, and originality
- Practice transparent documentation of AI usage

## Instructions

### Part 1: AI-Assisted Writing (20 points)

Choose ONE of the following writing tasks:

**Option A — Research Summary:** Pick an academic article (any field) published in 2024 or later. Use AI to help you write a 750-word summary suitable for a general audience. Document every prompt you used.

**Option B — Professional Document:** Use AI to help draft a cover letter, personal statement, or professional bio for yourself. Go through at least 3 rounds of AI-assisted revision.

**Option C — Creative Piece:** Write a 500-word creative piece (short story, poem, blog post) collaborating with AI. You must contribute original ideas and direction — AI is your co-writer, not your ghostwriter.

**For whichever option you choose, submit:**
1. The final document
2. A log of every AI prompt and response used in the process
3. A 300-word "process reflection" describing: What did you write vs. what did AI write? How did you guide the AI? What did you reject or edit from the AI's suggestions? Would the reader know AI was involved?

### Part 2: AI-Assisted Code (20 points)

**No programming experience needed.** Complete both exercises:

**Exercise A — Code Generation:**
Ask an AI to write a simple program that does ONE of the following:
- Converts temperatures between Fahrenheit and Celsius
- Generates a random password of specified length
- Counts word frequency in a paragraph of text

Submit: your prompt, the generated code, and a line-by-line explanation (ask the AI to explain each line, then verify you understand it).

### Part 3: Quality Evaluation (10 points)

Write a 400-word evaluation addressing:
1. **Accuracy:** Did the AI produce any factual errors or questionable code? How did you verify?
2. **Style:** How natural was the AI-generated writing? Could a reader tell?
3. **Efficiency:** Did AI save you time? Where was it most/least helpful?
4. **Limitations:** What couldn't the AI do well? What still required your judgment?
