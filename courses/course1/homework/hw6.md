# Homework 6: Personal AI Toolkit Design

**GENAI 101 — Foundations of Generative AI**
**Due:** End of Week 14 | **Points:** 50 (5% of final grade)

---

## Learning Objectives

- Synthesize course concepts into a practical, personalized workflow
- Evaluate and select AI tools for specific professional/academic tasks
- Design repeatable processes that integrate AI responsibly
- Prepare for the final presentation (Week 15)

## Instructions

### Part 1: Needs Assessment (10 points)

Identify your primary context (your major, job, or career goal). Then create a detailed inventory of **8–10 tasks** you regularly perform that could benefit from AI assistance. For each task:
- Describe the task (1-2 sentences)
- Estimate time currently spent
- Rate AI potential (High/Medium/Low) with a one-sentence justification

Present this as a table or structured list.

### Part 2: Tool Selection (15 points)

Research and select AI tools for your top 5 tasks. For each:
- **Tool name and type** (e.g., ChatGPT for drafting, Grammarly for editing, GitHub Copilot for code)
- **Why this tool** over alternatives (reference your HW1 model comparison experience)
- **Cost** (free tier vs. paid — what do you get at each level?)
- **Privacy/security considerations** — what data does this tool collect? Is it appropriate for your use case?
- **Limitations** you need to watch for (reference your HW5 hallucination audit)

### Part 3: Workflow Design (15 points)

Design **3 complete workflows** that integrate AI into real tasks. Each workflow should include:

1. **Trigger** — What initiates this workflow?
2. **Steps** — A numbered sequence including which AI tool is used, what prompts/templates you'd use, and what human review is needed
3. **Quality check** — How do you verify the output?
4. **Ethical guardrails** — Disclosure requirements, data sensitivity, bias checks
5. **Fallback** — What do you do when the AI fails or is unavailable?

Present at least one workflow as a visual diagram (can be hand-drawn and scanned, or created digitally).

### Part 4: Implementation Test (5 points)

Actually use ONE of your workflows on a real task this week. Document:
- What you did
- What worked and what didn't
- How you'd revise the workflow based on experience

### Part 5: Presentation Outline (5 points)

Create a 1-page outline for your Week 15 presentation (5 minutes). Include:
- Key points you'll cover
- Demo you'll show (if any)
- How you'll structure your time

## Submission Format

- Single PDF or Word document
- Include all parts clearly labeled
- Visual diagram may be embedded or attached separately
- Submit via the course LMS by 11:59 PM on the due date
