# Homework 4: Ethics Case Study

**GENAI 101 — Foundations of Generative AI**
**Due:** End of Week 10 | **Points:** 50 (5% of final grade)

---

## Learning Objectives

- Analyze real-world ethical issues in generative AI deployment
- Apply ethical frameworks to technology decisions
- Evaluate institutional AI policies critically
- Develop your own reasoned position on AI ethics questions

## Instructions

### Part 1: Case Selection & Research (10 points)

Choose ONE real-world AI ethics case from the list below (or propose your own with instructor approval):

1. **CNET's AI-Generated Articles (2023)** — A news outlet published AI-written articles with factual errors without disclosure
2. **Deepfake Political Ads (2023-2024)** — AI-generated audio/video used in election campaigns
3. **AI Art & Copyright (Midjourney/Stable Diffusion lawsuits)** — Artists suing AI companies over training data
4. **Amazon's AI Hiring Tool Bias** — An AI resume screener that discriminated against women
5. **AI-Generated Student Submissions** — The rise of AI detection tools and false accusations
6. **GitHub Copilot & Open Source Licensing** — AI trained on open-source code; who owns the output?

Research your chosen case using at least 3 credible sources (academic papers, major news outlets, official reports). Summarize the case in 400–500 words, including: what happened, who was affected, what the outcome was (or current status).

### Part 2: Ethical Analysis (20 points)

Analyze your case through **two** of the following ethical lenses:

1. **Utilitarianism** — Does this technology produce the greatest good for the greatest number? Who benefits? Who is harmed?
2. **Deontological Ethics** — Are there moral rules or duties being violated regardless of outcome? (e.g., right to informed consent, intellectual property rights)
3. **Virtue Ethics** — What does this case tell us about the character/values of the actors involved? What would a virtuous technologist do?
4. **Justice/Fairness** — Are the benefits and burdens distributed equitably? Who is disproportionately affected?

For each framework: apply it to your case in 300–400 words. State what each framework would conclude and whether you agree.

### Part 3: Policy Proposal (15 points)

Draft a 500-word policy proposal that addresses the ethical issue in your case. Your policy should:
- Identify the specific problem it solves
- Propose concrete, actionable rules or guidelines
- Consider enforcement mechanisms
- Address potential objections or unintended consequences
- Be realistic (could actually be implemented by an organization, university, or government)

### Part 4: Reflection (5 points)

In 200 words: Has studying this case changed your personal stance on AI ethics? What's the hardest ethical trade-off you've encountered in thinking about generative AI?

## Submission Format

- Single PDF or Word document, 2000–2500 words total
- Include bibliography with at least 3 sources (APA or MLA format)
- Submit via the course LMS by 11:59 PM on the due date
