# Homework 1: Problem Statement & Stakeholder Analysis

## GENAI 490 — Capstone: AI Solution Design

**Milestone Deliverable 1 of 6** | **Due: Week 2** | **100 Points**

---

## Overview

This is the foundation of your capstone project. You will identify a real-world problem suitable for a generative AI solution, articulate it clearly, and map the stakeholder landscape. This document becomes the north star for every subsequent deliverable.

## Learning Objectives

By completing this assignment, you will be able to:

1. Identify and scope a business or societal problem amenable to generative AI
2. Write a precise, falsifiable problem statement
3. Conduct stakeholder analysis including power/interest mapping
4. Articulate success criteria and key constraints
5. Assess ethical implications at the problem-definition stage

## Instructions

### Part 1: Problem Identification (20 pts)

Write a **1-page problem brief** that includes:

- **Domain context** — What industry or domain? Why now?
- **Current state** — How is this problem handled today? What are the pain points?
- **Opportunity** — Why is generative AI a viable approach (vs. traditional ML, rule-based systems, or manual processes)?
- **Scope boundary** — What is explicitly in-scope and out-of-scope for your capstone?

### Part 2: Problem Statement (15 pts)

Craft a single, concise problem statement using this template:

> **[Stakeholder group]** needs a way to **[core need]** because **[insight/evidence]**. A successful solution would **[measurable outcome]**.

Then provide:

- 2–3 supporting data points or references justifying the problem's significance
- A brief explanation of why existing solutions are insufficient

### Part 3: Stakeholder Analysis (35 pts)

Identify **at least 5 stakeholders** (individuals, groups, or organizations) and for each provide:

| Element | Description |
|---|---|
| **Stakeholder** | Name/role |
| **Interest** | What they care about regarding this problem |
| **Influence** | High/Medium/Low — their power over the project |
| **Impact** | High/Medium/Low — how much the solution affects them |
| **Needs & Concerns** | What they require; what worries them |
| **Engagement Strategy** | How you will involve them |

Include a **Power/Interest Grid** (2×2 matrix) plotting all stakeholders.

### Part 4: Success Criteria & Constraints (15 pts)

Define:

- **3–5 measurable success criteria** (e.g., "Reduce average document processing time from 45 min to under 10 min")
- **Technical constraints** (data availability, compute, latency requirements)
- **Organizational constraints** (budget, timeline, team skills, regulatory)
- **Ethical constraints** (bias risks, privacy, consent, transparency)

### Part 5: Ethical Pre-Assessment (15 pts)

Conduct a preliminary ethical review:

- Who could be **harmed** by this solution? How?
- What **biases** might exist in training data or model outputs?
- What **transparency and explainability** requirements apply?
- What is your proposed **mitigation strategy** for the top 3 risks?

## Submission Requirements

- **Format:** PDF or Markdown, 4–6 pages (excluding references)
- **References:** Minimum 3 credible sources (industry reports, academic papers, or official documentation)
- **Appendix (optional):** Interview notes, survey data, or additional research

## Rubric (100 Points)

| Criterion | Excellent (90-100%) | Proficient (70-89%) | Developing (50-69%) | Insufficient (<50%) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Problem Identification (20)** | Clear, well-researched domain context; compelling case for GenAI | Adequate context; reasonable GenAI justification | Vague context; weak GenAI rationale | Missing or incoherent problem identification |
| **Problem Statement (15)** | Precise, falsifiable, evidence-backed | Clear but lacks supporting evidence | Ambiguous or overly broad | Missing or unusable |
| **Stakeholder Analysis (35)** | 5+ stakeholders with thorough analysis; insightful power/interest grid | 5 stakeholders with adequate detail; grid present | Fewer than 5 or superficial analysis | Missing or minimal stakeholder work |
| **Success Criteria & Constraints (15)** | Measurable, realistic criteria; comprehensive constraint mapping | Criteria present but some lack measurability | Vague criteria; incomplete constraints | Missing or unrealistic |
| **Ethical Pre-Assessment (15)** | Thoughtful harm analysis; concrete mitigations | Identifies risks but mitigations are generic | Surface-level ethical consideration | Missing or dismissive |
