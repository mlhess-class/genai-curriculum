# Homework 6: Final Portfolio Draft

## GENAI 490 — Capstone: AI Solution Design

**Milestone Deliverable 6 of 6** | **Due: Week 14** | **100 Points**

---

## Overview

Assemble your capstone portfolio — the comprehensive artifact that tells the story of your project from problem to solution. This draft will be reviewed by peers and the instructor before the final presentation. Treat this as a professional portfolio piece suitable for job interviews or client presentations.

## Learning Objectives

1. Synthesize an entire AI project lifecycle into a coherent narrative
2. Demonstrate professional documentation and presentation skills
3. Reflect critically on design decisions, trade-offs, and lessons learned
4. Produce a portfolio-quality artifact for professional use
5. Integrate peer feedback into final deliverables

## Instructions

### Part 1: Portfolio Narrative (25 pts)

Write a **cohesive project narrative** (not just stapled-together homework). Structure:

1. **The Problem** — Refined from HW1, incorporating what you learned since
2. **The Approach** — How you tackled it; key design decisions and why
3. **The Solution** — What you built; how it works; architecture highlights
4. **The Evidence** — User testing results and business case summary
5. **The Reflection** — What you'd do differently; what surprised you; what you're proud of

This should read as a single document, not six summaries glued together. Aim for 6–8 pages.

### Part 2: Technical Portfolio (25 pts)

Compile and polish:

- **Architecture diagram** (final, updated version)
- **System demo** — Updated screen recording or live link showing the refined prototype
- **Code repository** — Clean, documented, with a proper README
- **API documentation** (if applicable)
- **Key prompts and evaluation results** — Your best prompt engineering work with before/after comparisons

### Part 3: Impact & Outcomes (20 pts)

Summarize the evidence:

- **User testing metrics** — Final quantitative results
- **Business case summary** — One-page version of HW5's key findings
- **Comparison to success criteria** — How does your solution measure against the criteria from HW1?
- **Limitations and honest assessment** — What doesn't work well? What would need more work for production?

### Part 4: Lessons Learned & Professional Reflection (15 pts)

Write a reflective essay (2–3 pages):

- **Technical lessons** — What you learned about building with generative AI
- **Process lessons** — What you learned about AI product development
- **Ethical reflections** — How your ethical thinking evolved from HW1 to now
- **Career impact** — How this project has shaped your understanding of AI in industry
- **Advice to future students** — What would you tell someone starting this capstone?

### Part 5: Peer Review Readiness (15 pts)

Prepare for peer review:

- **Self-assessment** — Score your own project using the final rubric (exams/final-rubric.md); justify each score
- **3 specific questions** for peer reviewers — What feedback would be most valuable?
- **Presentation outline** — Slide-by-slide plan for your final presentation (15 min talk + 5 min Q&A)

## Submission Requirements

- **Format:** PDF or Markdown, 12–18 pages total (including reflection, excluding appendices)
- **Repository:** Final GitHub link with clean code and README
- **Demo:** Updated screen recording or live URL
- **Appendices:** All prior homework deliverables (revised) as appendices
