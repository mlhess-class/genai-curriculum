# Homework 4: User Testing Findings & Iteration Plan

## GENAI 490 — Capstone: AI Solution Design

**Milestone Deliverable 4 of 6** | **Due: Week 10** | **100 Points**

---

## Overview

Test your prototype with real users from your target stakeholder group. Synthesize findings into actionable insights and create a concrete iteration plan. This assignment bridges building and refining — the hallmark of professional AI product development.

## Learning Objectives

1. Design and conduct user testing sessions for AI-powered products
2. Synthesize qualitative and quantitative user feedback
3. Identify usability and trust issues specific to generative AI interfaces
4. Prioritize improvements using evidence-based frameworks
5. Create an actionable iteration plan with clear success metrics

## Instructions

### Part 1: Test Plan Design (15 pts)

Before testing, document:

- **Target users** — Who are you testing with? (minimum 5 participants) How do they represent your stakeholder groups?
- **Testing methodology** — Moderated/unmoderated; think-aloud; task-based; interview
- **Task scenarios** — 3–5 realistic tasks that exercise core functionality
- **Data collection plan** — What you'll measure (task completion, time-on-task, satisfaction, trust ratings, error rates)
- **AI-specific observation criteria** — How users react to AI-generated content (trust, verification behavior, surprise, confusion)

### Part 2: Testing Execution & Raw Findings (30 pts)

Conduct your sessions and report:

- **Participant profiles** (anonymized) — Role, AI experience level, domain expertise
- **Session summaries** — Key observations per participant (1 paragraph each)
- **Quantitative data** — Task completion rates, time-on-task, satisfaction scores (SUS or similar)
- **Qualitative data** — Notable quotes, behavioral patterns, emotional reactions
- **AI trust observations** — Did users trust the AI output? Over-trust? Under-trust? How did they verify?

### Part 3: Findings Synthesis (30 pts)

Analyze your data:

1. **Affinity mapping** — Group observations into themes (include photo or digital version of your affinity map)
2. **Top 5 findings** — Ranked by severity and frequency, each with:
   - Description of the issue or insight
   - Supporting evidence (quotes, data points, observation counts)
   - Severity rating (Critical / Major / Minor / Enhancement)
   - Number of participants affected
3. **AI-specific findings** — Dedicate a section to:
   - Output quality perception
   - Trust calibration issues
   - Explainability gaps
   - Edge cases users discovered

### Part 4: Iteration Plan (25 pts)

Based on findings, create a prioritized plan:

- **Prioritization matrix** — Plot findings on Impact vs. Effort (2×2)
- **Sprint 2 scope** — What you will fix/improve before the final deliverable
- **Detailed action items** — For each planned change:
  - What changes (UI, prompt, model, pipeline, etc.)
  - Expected impact on user experience
  - How you'll validate the improvement
- **What you're deferring** — Items you won't address and why (time, scope, feasibility)
- **Updated success criteria** — Revised metrics based on what you learned

## Submission Requirements

- **Format:** PDF or Markdown, 6–10 pages
- **Appendices (required):**
  - Test script/protocol
  - Raw data tables
  - Consent forms (template is fine; don't include signed copies)
  - Affinity map photo/screenshot
