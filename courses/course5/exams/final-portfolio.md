# Final Portfolio Requirements

## GENAI 490 — Capstone: AI Solution Design

**Final Exam Equivalent** | **Due: Week 15** | **200 Points Total (150 portfolio + 50 presentation)**

## Overview

The final portfolio and presentation replace a traditional final exam. You will submit a polished version of your HW6 draft (incorporating peer and instructor feedback) and deliver a 15-minute presentation to a panel of faculty and industry professionals.

## Portfolio Requirements

### Deliverable Checklist

Your portfolio must include all of the following. Missing components receive zero for that section.

- [ ] **Portfolio Document** (PDF, 15–20 pages excluding appendices)
  - Project narrative (problem → approach → solution → evidence → reflection)
  - Updated from HW6 draft based on peer/instructor feedback
  - Professional formatting, clear headings, page numbers

- [ ] **Technical Artifacts**
  - Final architecture diagram (high-resolution, labeled)
  - Working demo: live URL **or** screen recording (10–15 min, narrated)
  - GitHub repository (public or instructor-shared) with:
    - Clean, documented code
    - Professional README (setup instructions, architecture overview, tech stack)
    - Prompt library with versioning
    - Test suite with results

- [ ] **Evidence Package**
  - User testing report (final version, incorporating HW4 feedback)
  - Evaluation results: minimum 15 test cases with outcomes
  - Business case summary (2-page version of HW5)
  - Success criteria comparison table (HW1 targets vs. actual outcomes)

- [ ] **Reflection Essay** (3–4 pages)
  - Technical lessons learned
  - Process and methodology insights
  - Ethical evolution (how your thinking changed from Week 1 to Week 15)
  - Career impact and professional growth
  - Advice for future students

- [ ] **Appendices**
  - All 6 homework deliverables (revised final versions)
  - Raw user testing data
  - Cost model spreadsheet
  - Prompt engineering log (showing iteration history)

### Quality Standards

- **Writing:** Professional, clear, free of grammatical errors. This should be portfolio-quality work you'd share with an employer.
- **Diagrams:** High-resolution, properly labeled, using standard notation (UML, C4, or equivalent)
- **Code:** Follows language conventions; includes comments for non-obvious logic; passes linting
- **Data:** All claims backed by evidence; assumptions numbered and traceable

## Presentation Requirements

### Format

- **Duration:** 15 minutes presentation + 5 minutes Q&A
- **Audience:** Faculty panel + industry guests (assume technical familiarity but not domain expertise)
- **Slides:** 12–18 slides maximum

### Required Sections

1. **Problem & Opportunity** (2 min) — Hook the audience; make them care
2. **Solution Demo** (4 min) — Live demo preferred; recorded backup required
3. **Technical Architecture** (3 min) — Key decisions and trade-offs
4. **Evidence & Outcomes** (3 min) — User testing, evaluation, business case highlights
5. **Lessons & Reflection** (2 min) — What you learned; what you'd do differently
6. **Q&A** (5 min) — Be prepared for technical, business, and ethical questions

### Presentation Tips

- Open with a story or concrete example, not a slide full of bullet points
- Demo should show the core workflow, not every feature
- Anticipate questions: "Why not fine-tune instead of RAG?" "How does this handle [edge case]?" "What's the unit economics?"
- Practice your timing — going over 15 minutes loses points

## Academic Integrity

- All work must be your own. You may use AI tools in your project (that's the point), but the portfolio narrative, reflection, and analysis must be your original writing.
- Cite all sources. Use AI tools transparently — document where and how you used them in your development process.
